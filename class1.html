Book Value vs Market Value

📖 What's the Difference?

Book Value: Assets - Liabilities (what's on the balance sheet)

Market Value: What investors think the company is worth (stock price × shares)

Why they differ: Balance sheets only show tangible assets at historical cost. They miss intangibles like brand value, patents, customer relationships, and future earnings potential.

🥤 Example: Coca-Cola

Book Value: Low - Some factories, but franchises most bottling (franchisees own those assets)

Market Value: MASSIVE - Brand worth $80B+, secret formula, global distribution, customer loyalty

The Gap: Coke's value is in BRAND and FORMULA, not physical assets. Book value doesn't capture that.

The Cupcake Problem ⭐

📋 The Setup

You start a cupcake business and:

  1. Deposit $3,000 cash
  2. Buy an oven for $1,000
  3. Buy ingredients for $500
  4. Receive $800 advance for a wedding order you'll fulfill NEXT quarter

Questions: How much CASH? How much REVENUE?

🎓 Watch Me Solve It

STEP 1: Calculate CASH 
Starting cash:           $3,000
Buy oven (cash out):    -$1,000
Buy ingredients:          -$500
Advance payment:          +$800
─────────────────────────────
CASH ON HAND:            $2,300
STEP 2: Calculate REVENUE 
The $800 advance:
- Have we EARNED it? NO
- Delivered cupcakes? NO
- Fulfilled order? NO

→ NOT revenue yet!

TOTAL REVENUE:              $0

Key Point: Cash = $2,300 but Revenue = $0. The $800 is a LIABILITY (unearned revenue) until you deliver.

The Three Financial Statements

1. Balance Sheet

Core Question: What do you OWN and what do you OWE?

Formula: Assets = Liabilities + Equity

Timing: Snapshot at a point in time

2. Income Statement (P&L)

Core Question: Are you making MONEY?

Revenue
- Cost of Goods Sold
= Gross Profit
- Operating Expenses
= Net Income

Timing: Period of time (year, quarter)

3. Cash Flow Statement

Core Question: Where did the CASH actually go?

Three sections: Operations, Investing, Financing

Key Insight: You can be profitable but still run out of cash!
