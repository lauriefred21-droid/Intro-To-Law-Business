Pre-Money vs Post-Money Valuation

The Core Concepts

Pre-Money Valuation: What the company is worth BEFORE the new investment

Post-Money Valuation: What the company is worth AFTER the new investment

The Formula:

Post-Money = Pre-Money + Investment Amount

OR

Investment Amount ÷ Ownership % = Post-Money Valuation

Simple Example

VC wants to invest $5M for 25% of the company.

Post-Money Valuation:
$5M ÷ 25% = $20M

Pre-Money Valuation:
$20M - $5M = $15M

So the company was worth $15M before,
and will be worth $20M after the investment.

THE SERIES B PROBLEM ⭐⭐⭐

THIS IS THE BIG ONE! Master this calculation. It WILL be on the exam with different numbers.

The Setup

Current Cap Table:

  • Common stockholders: 10,000,000 shares
  • Series A investors: 5,000,000 shares (invested $5M at $1.00/share)
  • Total current shares: 15,000,000

Series B wants to invest:

  • Investment amount: $10,000,000
  • For: 50% ownership

Questions:

  1. How many total shares must be outstanding after Series B for them to own exactly 50%?
  2. How many new shares does Series B receive?
  3. What is the price per share for Series B?
  4. After Series B, what % does Common own? What % does Series A own?

STEP 1: Total Shares After Series B

If Series B owns 50%, then the other 50% is everyone else.

Currently: 15M shares (Common + Series A)
These 15M shares will be 50% after the deal.

If 15M = 50%
Then Total Shares = 15M ÷ 0.5 = 30M shares

Answer: 30,000,000 total shares after Series B

STEP 2: Shares for Series B

Total shares after: 30M
Existing shares: 15M
New shares issued: 30M - 15M = 15M

Answer: Series B receives 15,000,000 new shares

STEP 3: Price Per Share

Series B investment: $10,000,000
Series B shares: 15,000,000

Price per share = $10M ÷ 15M = $0.67 per share

Answer: $0.67 per share

Note: This is a DOWN ROUND (price went from $1.00 to $0.67)

STEP 4: Final Ownership Percentages

Total shares: 30,000,000

Common: 10M ÷ 30M = 33.3%
Series A: 5M ÷ 30M = 16.7%
Series B: 15M ÷ 30M = 50.0%

Check: 33.3% + 16.7% + 50% = 100% ✓

The Complete Answer

  1. Total shares after Series B: 30,000,000
  2. New shares to Series B: 15,000,000
  3. Price per share: $0.67
  4. Final ownership:
    • Common: 33.3%
    • Series A: 16.7%
    • Series B: 50%

How to Solve ANY Series B Type Problem

  1. Find current total shares (add up all existing shareholders)
  2. Set up the equation: Current shares = (1 - New investor %) × Total after
  3. Solve for total after: Total = Current ÷ (1 - New %)
  4. New shares: Total after - Current
  5. Price per share: Investment ÷ New shares
  6. Final %s: Each shareholder's shares ÷ Total

Liquidation Preferences

What Is a Liquidation Preference?

Definition: VCs get paid back their investment (or a multiple) BEFORE common stockholders get anything when the company exits.

Why VCs Want This: Downside protection. If company sells for less than hoped, VCs at least get their money back.

Types of Liquidation Preferences

1. Non-Participating Preferred

VC chooses EITHER:

  • Get liquidation preference (1X or more), OR
  • Convert to common and get pro-rata share

Whichever is higher!

2. Participating Preferred

VC gets BOTH:

  • Liquidation preference back, AND
  • Pro-rata share of remaining proceeds

(Much better for VCs, worse for founders)

Non-Participating Example

Setup:

  • VC invested: $1M
  • Pre-money valuation: $4M
  • Post-money: $5M
  • VC owns: 20% ($1M ÷ $5M)
  • Liquidation preference: 1.5X ($1.5M)

Scenario 1: Company Sells for $2M

Option A - Take liquidation preference:
VC gets: $1.5M (their 1.5X preference)
Common gets: $0.5M (what's left)

Option B - Convert to common and take pro-rata:
VC gets: $2M × 20% = $400K
Common gets: $2M × 80% = $1.6M

VC chooses: Option A ($1.5M > $400K)
Result: VC gets $1.5M, Common gets $500K

Scenario 2: Company Sells for $10M

Option A - Take liquidation preference:
VC gets: $1.5M
Common gets: $8.5M

Option B - Convert to common:
VC gets: $10M × 20% = $2M
Common gets: $10M × 80% = $8M

VC chooses: Option B ($2M > $1.5M)
Result: VC gets $2M, Common gets $8M

The Pattern:

  • Low exit → VCs take the preference (downside protection)
  • High exit → VCs convert to common (upside participation)

Anti-Dilution Protection

What it is: Protection for VCs if company raises money at a lower valuation (down round)

When it kicks in: Series B price < Series A price

Who bears the cost: Common stockholders (founders) get diluted more to give VCs extra shares

Not tested: You do NOT need to know how to calculate anti-dilution adjustments

Review Questions (From Professor)

Question 1

Be able to compute the pre-and post-money valuation of a firm based on an investment amount and share of the company.

Formula:

Investment Amount ÷ Ownership % = Post-Money

Post-Money - Investment = Pre-Money

Example: $8M for 40%

Post-Money = $8M ÷ 0.40 = $20M
Pre-Money = $20M - $8M = $12M
